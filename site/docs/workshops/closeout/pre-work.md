# Closeout Pre-Work

Complete this before the Closeout session (or before you start the Closeout worksheets
self-paced). The goal is to arrive with your work assembled and at least a first draft of your
learnings written — so the session is about refining and sharing, not reconstructing what you did.

---

## Step 1 — Assemble what you built

Before you can close a project, you need to see it in one place. Gather the artifacts from the
Building part so you are not hunting for them during the session.

**Collect and confirm you can open each of the following:**

- [ ] Your project's repository, on the branch you worked on during Building
- [ ] Your **plan** from the Planning part (the PRD and the decomposed task list)
- [ ] Your **execution and verification worksheet** from the Building part — the record of which
      tasks you ran autonomously, what the diffs showed, and what your verification command returned
- [ ] Your **quality-gate worksheet** from the Building part — your gate command, what it checks,
      and the defect class it misses
- [ ] Any notes you took during the Building exercises

If any of these are missing, locate them or reconstruct them before continuing. The Closeout
worksheets ask you to reflect on specific things you did — not a general impression.

**What you built (fill this in):**

> Looking at your plan and your execution worksheet, describe in two or three sentences what you
> actually produced during Building. What exists now that did not exist before? If the scope changed
> from your original plan, note what shifted and why.

## Step 2 — Draft your key learnings

The Closeout session starts with a closing ceremony where each participant shares one thing they are
proud of and one thing they would do differently. Writing both before the session means you arrive
with a real answer, not a placeholder.

**Work through these prompts now, before the session:**

**The thing you are most proud of:**

> What worked better than you expected — either something you built, a decision you made, or
> a way the process went? Be specific. "The worktree setup went smoothly" is more useful than
> "it went well."

## Step 3 — Surface the context worth carrying forward

The carry-forward artifact you will produce during the Closeout session is a structured document
capturing what future work should reuse, what it should avoid, and what you now know about this
kind of work that you did not know at the start.

Before the session, think through these questions and write your first-draft answers. You will
refine them during the session using the
[learnings-capture and carry-forward worksheet](./learnings-carry-forward-worksheet.md).

**Patterns that worked:**

> What approach or practice would you carry directly into your next project — not just "I would
> do this again" but why it worked and when you think it would transfer.

## Step 4 — Prepare your ceremony contribution

The closing ceremony is a brief round — each participant shares one thing they are proud of and one
thing they would do differently. Your answers from Step 2 are your ceremony contribution.

For a **live cohort**: bring your answers written and openable so you are not composing on the spot.

For **self-paced readers**: the ceremony is a written reflection you complete before opening the
learnings-capture worksheet. Write your answers in Step 2, then treat the act of finishing this
pre-work as the ceremony moment before you continue.

---

## Ready check

Before you begin the Closeout session or open the first worksheet:

- [ ] You have assembled your repository, plan, and worksheets from Building (Step 1 above)
- [ ] You have described what you built in two or three sentences (Step 1 above)
- [ ] Your ceremony contribution — what you are proud of and what you would do differently — is
      written (Step 2 above)
- [ ] Your first-draft learnings and context are written: patterns, dead ends, and one thing you
      now understand (Step 3 above)

If you are joining a live cohort, bring this page with the prompts filled. If you are working
self-paced, keep it open alongside the
[learnings-capture and carry-forward worksheet](./learnings-carry-forward-worksheet.md) — that
worksheet is your next step.
